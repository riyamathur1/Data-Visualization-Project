Introduction

In the United States, it's estimated that police officers fatally shoot over 1,000 individuals each year. Since 2015, there have been approximately 10,000 instances of police-involved shootings. There is no mandate for local police departments to report these incidents to higher authorities, leading to a significant lack of accountability. Recognizing the importance of transparent information in this area, the Washington Post has documented each person shot and killed by police officers since 2015. Police Shootings Trends Across the Years


Since the killing of George Floyd in May 2020, protests have erupted nationwide against police violence. Despite this, the data indicates that there has been no decline in such incidents since then.

Black People are killed at much higher rates


While White Americans make up over half the victims of police shootings, African Americans are killed at a much higher rate. They account for around 14% of the population, yet are killed at over twice the rate of White Americans.

Most of the victims are young

Over half the people shot by police in this timeframe are under 40 years old.
